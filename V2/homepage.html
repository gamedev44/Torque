‚ñ∂ ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What's the main difference between Torque.JS and Engine Simulator?
Note: This comparison refers to the Engine Simulator open source edition available on GitHub, not the Community Edition or upcoming 3D version. While both tools may look visually similar, Torque.JS uses pure tone generation (synthesis) instead of sound files. This means you can create any engine sound that can be simulated, with full control over every parameter. The Engine Simulator open source edition uses pre-recorded sound files (available in the sound library), which limits customization to what's available in the library.
Do I need audio engineering knowledge to use Torque.JS?
No! Torque.JS is designed for effortless creation. The intuitive dashboard interface allows you to create realistic engine sounds without complex audio understanding. Just load a preset and start tweaking!
Is Torque.JS as robust as Engine Simulator?
Note: This comparison refers to the Engine Simulator open source edition, not the Community Edition or upcoming 3D version. Torque.JS is still growing, but it's promising for its ease of use. While the Engine Simulator open source edition may have more features currently, Torque.JS offers something unique: full control over every aspect of the sound, allowing you to simulate anything that can be mathematically generated.
Can I customize everything in Torque.JS?
Yes! Unlike tools with limited customization, Torque.JS gives you complete control over pitch, harmonics, filters, distortion, and more. You're not limited to pre-existing sound files - you can create anything that can be simulated through synthesis.
